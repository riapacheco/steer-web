Getting Started

Introduction

Steer is a tool for business and team management. It simplifies work by making business goals (at all levels) easier to understand and thus easier to achieve. This allows you to focus your time on results and pull your team away from distraction. You can build goals at a high "priority management" level for a full top-down approach, or you can choose to include goals at the project level to practically manage your commitments, costs, and time.

Use Steer for handling those big business unknowns, for getting clarity for your remote team, and to handle the messy changes that comes with "being in this industry". If it's a challenge, Steer was built to overcome it.

Settings Menu

After you register your account and access the workspace, you'll immediately be given the option to follow a step-by-step tour of the product.

If you'd like to complete this tour later, you can access it by clicking your account photo or username in the bottom left corner, and selecting the Quick Tutorial option in your settings menu.

Workspace & Account Profile

To change the title that appears at the top left of your workspace screen, select Profile from that same settings menu and apply this change to the Workspace Name field.

Workspace Users

Navigate to User Settings

To add and manage your workspace team members, navigate to your settings menu and select Users

Add New Users

In the top-right corner, press the Add New button. An Add New User dialog will appear. All you need to add is their email, but you can also add their full name and professional title.

Guest Access

To make this a Guest User, switch the Enable Guest Access to Select Projects toggle to the ON position. A new field will appear directly under it. Begin typing the name of the project card you wish to give this user access to, and select it from the dropdown menu.

Guest users gain access to as many project cards as you allow them to, and will never have access to your priority cards unless you make them a workspace user. To make a guest user into a workspace user, simply switch this same toggle to the OFF position.

Learn more about Guest Projects, and other ways to grant access to them, in the Guest Projects section.

Archive Users

To archive a user, select their name from the User List from the main User Settings page. Click the settings menu button (...) in their profile window's top-right corner. Select the Archive Member option.




To unarchive users, switch the Archived List toggle in the top right of the User Settings page to the ON position. You can either click on the user's name to trigger an option window for unarchiving them, or right-click their name and select the Unarchive user option from the menu that appears.

Creating Cards

In Steer, you can create two types of cards: Priority Cards and Project Cards. Both have similar features with some key differences -- most notable difference being that Project Cards come from Priority Cards.

Priority Cards

Priority cards are the high-level bigger projects in your workspace. You can think of them like the initiatives for your business, quarterly targets for the year, or like the projects that have far too many moving pieces (of which need to be managed in one place).

Project Cards

By contrast, Project Cards are lower-level. They are the the projects or ongoing programs that have a small (and in most cases dedicated) team. In many cases, they may have a unique start and end date. In other cases they may be cyclical activities that are repeated year after year.

Adding Priority Cards

To create a priority card, select the Add priority card option at the bottom of your left navigation menu.

A window will appear that will allow you to name and describe your priority card.

Private Priority Cards

To make the priority card private, simply toggle the Make this card private toggle to the ON position. This makes it so that no one else in the workspace can see this priority card or the items you create within it.

You can make this card public later by switching this toggle to the ON position within the created priority card's main menu.
